AT A GLANCE

WHO SHOULD GET TESTED?

Screening or routine test

  • No routine test for Mycoplasma genitalium is recommended. The harmful role of M. genitalium is less definitive in women than it is in men. 10-30% of females have Mycoplasma living in the vagina without causing any damage

Females

  • style="list-style-type: disc;"Females who have symptoms including vaginal discharge, smell, or burning that cannot be attributed to more common infections (chlamydia, gonorrhea, BV, yeast)
  • Females with suspicion of PID (pelvic inflammatory disease)
  • Female who has recurrent urethritis, vaginitis or cervicitis

Males

  • Males who have female partners with symptoms caused by mycoplasma
  • Males who have urethritis symptoms and all other bacteria that might cause it are ruled out (chlamydia, gonorrhea, trichomoniasis)
  • Males with recurrent urethritis

TEST OF ACCURACY

This is the test accuracy of a nucleic acid-based test such as PCR:


  • Female vaginal swab: 98.5%
  • Female urine test: 81.5%
  • Male urethral (penile) swab: 98.2%
  • Male urine test: 98.2%

TEST TYPES

There are two types of mycoplasma tests: the swab test and the urine test.


  • Male
    • Urine and urethral swab
  • Female
    • Urine and vaginal swab

Both tests are NAAT (nucleic acid amplification) PCR tests.

TEST PREPARATION

For the highest accuracy, prepare in the following ways:


  • Urine test: Avoid urinating for two hours before a urine test
  • Vaginal swab: Avoid douching or using vaginal cream for 24 hours prior to the test
  • Penile swab: No preparation is needed

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How are Mycoplasma tests done?

    Female vaginal swab


    A doctor will collect a sample of fluid from the vagina and/or the cervix using a thin, sterile cotton swab. This may cause mild discomfort. If the area is infected and the mucosa is fragile, swabbing might cause minor spotting afterward, but this is uncommon.


    Remember: You should not use vaginal creams or douches 24 hours before being tested.

    Image source:
    Chlamydia / N. Gonorrhoeae RNA, TMA - Unisex Collection | Diagnostic Laboratory of Oklahoma. https://www.dlolab.com/chlamydia-n-gonorrhoeae-rna-tma-unisex-collection.

    Male urethral swab


    A doctor will insert a thin, sterile cotton swab into the tip of the penis, three-fourths of an inch into the urethra, to collect urethral fluid. The test takes 15 to 30 seconds. Mild discomfort is possible.

    Image source:
    Chlamydia / N. Gonorrhoeae RNA, TMA - Unisex Collection | Diagnostic Laboratory of Oklahoma. https://www.dlolab.com/chlamydia-n-gonorrhoeae-rna-tma-unisex-collection.

    Urine test


    A doctor will ask you to urinate into a cup. It should be a first catch urine sample (the initial portion of the urine stream).


    Remember: You should not urinate for at least two hours prior to the test.

  • Oral and rectal mycoplasma test

    Oral and rectal Mycoplasma genitalium tests are not commonly performed unless one partner is getting recurrent Mycoplasma genitalium urethritis.

  • How long does it take to get results?

    • Urine test results are usually back in seven business days
    • Swab test results are usually back in seven business days

  • Should the partners of those who tested positive and symptomatic for Mycoplasma genitalium be tested and treated?

    Yes. All partners of people who have tested positive for Mycoplasma genitalium should be tested and treated.

  • Is Mycoplasma genitalium considered an STD?

    Mycoplasma genitalium is part of the normal flora of many females, and it can live on top of the epithelial cells of females’ genitals. It is considered an STD since it was obtained sexually at some point.

  • What is the treatment for Mycoplasma?

    There are three different treatment options available depending on the situation and the allergy profile:

    • One dose of Azithromycin (1 gm)
    • 400 mg of Moxifloxacin twice a day for seven days

  • Where can I get tested for Mycoplasma?

    Not many places offer the Mycoplasma test since it is part of the normal flora. However, the test can be done in GYN clinics, urology clinics or our specialized STD office.

  • Should I tell my partner that I have Mycoplasma?

    You should notify your current partners about your Mycoplasma status and suggest that they undergo a medical examination and receive treatment if necessary. If you are not comfortable telling them, we can anonymously notify them on your behalf via a call, text message or email.

  • Can I get infected with Mycoplasma again?

    Yes. This is another reason you should notify your partner(s) and suggest that they be tested and treated.

  • Mycoplasma retesting after treatment

    Ureaplasma can be retested for three to four weeks after treatment. 30% to 40% of Mycoplasma genitalium can be resistant to one dose Azithromycin and resistance is growing.

  • What are the symptoms of Mycoplasma?

    About 70% of people who have Mycoplasma genitalium do not experience any symptoms. Among symptomatic patients, the most commonly reported symptoms are the following:


    In heterosexual men

    • Painful urination
    • Urethral discharge
    • Irritation of the penis

    In homosexual men

    • Painful urination
    • Urethral discharge
    • Irritation of the penis
    • Symptoms of proctitis (left-side abdominal pain, diarrhea, rectal bleeding, pain)

    In women

    • Bleeding after sex
    • Bleeding on non-period days
    • Abnormal discharge from the vagina
    • Pelvic pain

  • Will health insurance cover the testing?

    Yes, health insurance usually covers the doctor’s office visit, testing and treatment. If you have a high-deductible plan and want to know your exact contribution for the testing, please call your insurance company and find out your contribution for the primary care office visit and Mycoplasma testing

  • What is the cost without health insurance?

    If you do not have health insurance the cost of the office visit is $84 cash (or $90 credit) plus $60 for the testing. All tests are performed by certified labs.
