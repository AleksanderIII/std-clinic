AT A GLANCE

WHO SHOULD GET TESTED?

Only a person with a vagina can be tested for BV (Bacterial vaginosis).



Screening or routine test

Not necessary


Females:

  • With symptoms of vaginal discharge, odor or burning sensation
  • Who are pregnant and experiencing symptoms of vaginal discharge, odor or burning sensation

HOW ACCURATE TESTS?

  • Female vaginal swab: 95%

  • Vaginal pH test: 73%

TEST TYPES

Two types of BV tests are available: vaginal swab and pH test.

TEST PREPARATION

For accurate testing:


  • Vaginal swab: Avoid having sex, practicing douching and using vaginal creams for 24 hours prior to testing.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What can I expect during a BV (Bacterial vaginosis) test?

    Females vaginal swab

    The test should be performed by a doctor. Self-swabbing is also possible.

    Vaginal and/or cervical fluid will be collected with a cotton swab (the procedure is illustrated below).

    The procedure may cause mild discomfort or minor spotting afterwards.

    Remember: You should not practice douching or use vaginal creams for 24 hours prior to testing.

    Image source:
    Chlamydia / N. Gonorrhoeae RNA, TMA - Unisex Collection | Diagnostic Laboratory of Oklahoma. https://www.dlolab.com/chlamydia-n-gonorrhoeae-rna-tma-unisex-collection.

    pH test

    A pH strip will be inserted, placed against the vaginal wall for a few seconds, and removed. The new color of the strip will be compared against a chart color to determine the pH level. A pH level > 4.5 suggests imbalanced vaginal flora, indicating possible BV.

  • When will I know my test results?

    • pH test (point of care test): few minutes
    • Swab test: 4 business days

  • What if my test is positive?

    Gardnerella vaginalis (main bacteria causing BV) is a curable infection. However, if a patient does not experience symptoms, she is not advised to get treatment.


    According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), females who test positive for Gardnerella vaginitis should be tested for other sexually transmitted infections such as HIV , Gonorrhea , and Chlamydia .

  • How is BV treated?

    Gardnerella is treated with one of the following options:

    • Metronidazole oral tablets (500mg) for a week (twice a day)
    • Intravaginal application of Metronidazole gel 0.75% for 5 days (once a day)
    • Intravaginal application of Clindamycin cream 2% for 1 week (every night)

    It is important to abstain from alcohol during the treatment period (1 week) and for 24 hours after treatment completion. Only protected sex (use of condoms during sex) is recommended during the treatment period.

  • If I tested positive, should my partner(s) be tested and treated?

    • It is not recommended to treat male partners of females diagnosed with Gardnerella vaginitis
    • Only female partners need to be tested for Gardnerella vaginitis because of the high probability of infection
    • Both male and female partners should undergo testing for other sexually transmitted infections

  • Should I get tested again after treatment?

    After-treatment testing is recommended only for individuals who continue to experience symptoms of Gardnerella vaginitis.

  • What is the probability of being infected with BV again?

    About 80% of treated patients are reinfected. In 30% of cases, reinfection happens within three months of treatment completion.

  • What is the recommended treatment for Gardnerella vaginitis recurrence?

    For Gardnerella vaginitis recurrence, the course of therapy should be extended by an additional 3-7 days (10-14 total days of treatment).


    For multiple recurrences of symptomatic Gardnerella vaginitis, one of the following is recommended:

    • Metronidazole oral tablets (2g) along with Fluconazole (150mg) for half a year (once a month)
    • Intravaginal application of Metronidazole gel 0.75% for 4-6 months (twice a week)

  • What are the factors resulting in the harmful growth of Gardnerella vaginitis (bacteria causing BV)?

    Factors that increase the risk of excessive growth of Gardnerella are:

    • Frequent sexual activity
    • Suppressed immunity
    • Pregnancy
    • Douching
    • Multiple sexual partners
    • Sex with another female
    • Smoking
    • Overweight
    • Low estrogen levels
    • Intrauterine device

  • Where can I get tested for BV?

    The test can be done in OB/GYN clinics, primary care offices or our specialized STD clinic.

  • What are the symptoms of BV?

    Bacterial vaginosis (BV) is a clinical condition caused by an imbalance of “good” and “bad” bacteria in the vagina.

    • 50 to 75% of females who have BV do not have symptoms
    • Those who have symptoms present with thin vaginal discharge with an off-white color and fishy smell, particularly after a sexual encounter and during a menstrual period
    • BV alone does not cause urinary symptoms (urgency, burning sensation while peeing, increased frequency), vaginal itchiness or swelling. The presence of those symptoms suggests an additional infection

  • Is Bacterial Vaginosis considered an STD?

    BV can be (and usually is) sexually transmitted but is not classified as an STD because of the lack of a single cause (only caused by sex) and absence of the disease in males.

  • Can I suspect my partner of infidelity if I have BV?

    No. Sexual activity, as well as many other factors (douching, pregnancy, hormone disbalance, oral contraceptive pill, etc.), can trigger Bacterial vaginosis.

  • What can I do to decrease the chances of getting BV?

    • Avoid douching
    • Limit the number of sexual partners (long-term sexual partnership leads to stability and alignment of vaginal micro flora)
    • Practice safe sex

  • What can I do to alleviate BV symptoms?

    Most people with Bacterial vaginosis (BV) have no symptoms.


    Those who have significant symptoms such as smelly discharge or itchiness need to be seen by a doctor.


    In the meantime, the following can help with managing symptoms:

    • Take a shower instead of a bath
    • Avoid vaginal washes and deodorant. Use regular soap and water instead
    • Wear breathable underwear (cotton)
