What you see (chlamydia signs)

In this section, we’ll talk about the signs of chlamydia—what you can see when you have chlamydia.


Knowing the signs should help you with early chlamydia diagnosis and treatment. Signs, unlike symptoms, are objective, which is why they are more trusted by doctors. Signs are things you and others see due to the disease process. Signs usually follow symptoms or occur at the same time. Here we summarize in tables chlamydia signs in the genital, oral, and rectal areas. Then you will see frequently asked questions that are answered by an expert STD doctor.

Chlamydia discharge

Here we will describe chlamydia discharge when the patient has no other conditions. *

Color

Chlamydia discharge is usually colorless to white.

Consistency

It’s usually very thin and watery. If the consistency is thicker or changing over time, most likely there’s another STD present as well.

Amount

Chlamydia infection does not cause significant inflammation, so the discharge is scanty.

  • Males usually see minimal discharge first thing in the morning.
  • Females might not notice it at all; they’re usually diagnosed during a gynecological exam or testing.

Smell

Chlamydia discharge usually doesn’t smell unless another condition is also present.

*Chlamydia discharge can be different in different people. And it can change if the patient also has other conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

In this section, our expert doctor will answer real-life patient questions about chlamydia signs. Most questions are grouped to make it easier to cover more topics. Dr. Fuzayloff has been a practicing physician at a busy STD center in Midtown Manhattan, New York City, for over two decades.

  • How are chlamydia signs used in establishing the diagnosis?

    Signs are essential to making a diagnosis. They enable us to choose the most accurate testing methods and to initiate treatment while awaiting test results. In general, they give us the right vector, so we understand in which direction we should go to evaluate further. However, we cannot establish the final diagnosis based only on the signs of disease. We consider them in combination with laboratory findings before establishing the diagnosis.


    Symptoms are subjective. They are sensations and experiences—you feel them rather than see them. Signs, on the other hand, are objective features of the disease—you can see them. And your healthcare provider can see them too. Therefore, signs are more reliable than symptoms.


    Both symptoms and signs should be analyzed in light of relevant circumstances:

    • How risky was the contact?
    • How much time passed before you felt symptoms?
    • Before you saw signs?
    • Are the symptoms logical and predictable?
    • Are the signs and symptoms progressing?

    We can make educated assumptions based on symptoms and signs, but we can never establish the exact diagnosis based solely on them. Our assumptions are important because they allow us to start the treatment process without delay. But testing is always necessary and advised.

  • What are the most reliable signs of chlamydia?

    We rely on signs that are either commonly seen or very specific to the disease and that have a clear, known explanation of their cause. When both of these criteria are met, a sign is referred to as reliable. Here are the most reliable signs for males and females and in the oral and rectal areas.

    Males (genital)

    The most reliable sign in a male is discharge from the penis, which can be yellowish, cloudy, milky, or transparent. It is caused by inflammation of the urethra.

    Females (genital)

    The most reliable signs in a female are:

    • Scanty discharge from the vagina
    • Bleeding after sexual intercourse
    • Bleeding between periods

    All these signs are explained by inflammation of the cervix (the part of the uterus that’s connected to the innermost part of the vagina)

    Oral

    You will usually notice no signs of oral chlamydia. We consider redness of the throat and tonsils to be the most reliable signs of the disease. They sometimes appear due to tissue damage caused by inflammation of the pharynx.

    Rectal

    In the case of proctitis (inflammation of the rectum), the most reliable signs are discharge and bleeding, which are caused by the inflammatory process in the rectal mucosa.
