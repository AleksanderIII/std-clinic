Chlamydia testing algorithms

Here we’ve summarized chlamydia testing for different common situations:

  • Screening for female
  • Screening for males
  • Testing after potential exposure when there are no symptoms
  • Testing when there are symptoms

Screening means that testing is done with no particular exposure in mind when the patient has no symptoms; it’s just a routine test.


Let’s look at the algorithms and see which is best for your particular situation.

Chlamydia screening recommendations for asymptomatic females

This screening algorithm is for females with no symptoms and no concern about a particular exposure.


Chlamydia screening recommendations for asymptomatic males

This screening algorithm is for males with no symptoms and no concern about a particular exposure.


Post-exposure chlamydia testing algorithm: with and without symptoms

This algorithm is for people who have had recent sexual exposure to a chlamydia-positive person or who currently have chlamydia symptoms .


*Please note that symptomatic people can get tested at any time.
