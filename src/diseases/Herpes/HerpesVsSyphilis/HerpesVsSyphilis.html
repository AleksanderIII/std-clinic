Herpes vs. syphilis (primary stage)

Here we’ll show you the differences between herpes and syphilis skin lesions, which look similar and are easily confused.

It’s important to distinguish between these two conditions:

  • Unlike herpes, syphilis may be easily cured if treated in time.
  • Unlike herpes, syphilis can have devastating health effects if it’s not treated.

Pay special attention to the following:

  • Herpes sores present as multiple clustered sores, while syphilis usually presents with one sore.
  • Herpes sores are small; syphilis sore are much bigger.
  • Herpes sores are very superficial (on the surface of the skin), but a syphilis sore is deep (firm when touched, with underlying firmness of the skin).
  • With herpes sores, their base is crusted; a syphilis sore has a clean base.
  • A syphilis sore’s border is hard to the touch and well defined, unlike herpes sores.

Frequently Asked Questions

In this frequently asked questions section, Dr. Fuzayloff answers in more detail questions about the most important points that differentiate between the two conditions.

  • How can a doctor tell the difference between herpes and syphilis?

    When a doctor examines a person with suspected herpes or syphilis, they try to put the signs and symptoms into the right context.


    What is the right context? It’s the history of the disease: what preceded the signs and symptoms, when they began appearing, how they’re evolving, and whether there are other associated symptoms.

    The time that’s passed since a risky encounter

    This is a very important point that distinguishes herpes from syphilis. Herpes blisters appear two to four days after the initial exposure. A syphilis sore, on the other hand, may take three or four weeks after exposure to appear.

    The number of sores

    Syphilis usually presents with solitary sore; very rarely, there are two to four lesions. The herpes infection presents as multiple lesions that usually are clustered and share a red base.

    The appearance of the sores

    To visually distinguish syphilis lesions from herpes lesions, follow the rule of three S’s:

    Surface

    • Syphilis lesions usually have a rough, clean surface and are not filled with fluid. Herpes lesions are very superficial blister (fluid-filled sacs) or crusted sores, depending on the stage.

    Shape

    • Syphilis lesions are usually round with indurated (hard) raised borders. Herpes lesions are very superficial and not felt with touch (there’s no underlying hardness of the skin).

    Size

    • Syphilis lesions are up to 3 cm in diameter. Herpes lesions are only 2–3 mm in diameter.

    Associated pain

    Unlike herpes lesions, a primary syphilis sore is not painful or itchy (that’s why it goes unnoticed).

    Duration of skin symptoms

    Syphilis has a longer incubation period and it’s longer before a syphilis sore becomes visible. And it lasts longer after it appears.


    With syphilis, the lesion might take up to two weeks to become visible and up to 8 weeks to heal. It might scar after healing since the lesion penetrates the entire skin thickness.


    Herpes lesions might take one or two days to appear and on average last for two weeks (this might extend to four weeks in the first outbreak). Since the lesions are superficial, no scarring is expected after healing.
