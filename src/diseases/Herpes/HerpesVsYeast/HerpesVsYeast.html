Herpes vs. vaginal yeast infection

This will help you tell the difference between herpes and a vaginal yeast infection.


Vaginal yeast infection is not an infection in itself, but rather a fungal overgrowth caused by moisture that can result from sweating, recent antibiotic intake, or discharge from another infection that keeps the area wet.


The skin lesions for these two conditions can look alike and may be easily confused. It’s important to distinguish these two conditions:

  • Unlike herpes, a vaginal yeast infection can be easily cured.
  • Unlike herpes, a vaginal yeast infection usually will not be transmitted to a partner.

Both can affect the vaginal area. Blister vs. redness

A vaginal yeast infection doesn’t usually cause blisters and ulcers.

Herpes presents with multiple blisters or sores on the same red base.

Discharge

A vaginal yeast infection produces a specific cottage cheese discharge.

Herpes has scanty clear discharge from the blisters with a thin consistency.

Entire area vs. one side

A vaginal yeast infection’s redness and irritation covers the entire vaginal and vulva area.

Herpes usually is on one side of the body and not as widespread.

Lymph node enlargement

A vaginal yeast infection rarely causes lymph node enlargement.
Herpes can cause localized lymph node enlargement on the side of the skin lesions.

Systemic symptoms

A vaginal yeast infection usually doesn’t have systemic symptoms but rather only vaginal irritation.

Herpes lesions can be preceded by fever, chills, and flu-like symptoms.

Frequently Asked Questions

In this frequently asked questions section, Dr. Fuzayloff answers in more detail questions about important points that differentiate herpes and vaginal yeast infection.

  • How can a doctor tell the difference between herpes and a vaginal yeast infection?

    When a doctor examines a person with suspected herpes or vaginal yeast infection, they try to put the signs and symptoms into the right context.


    What is the right context? It’s the history of the disease: what preceded it, when it began appearing, how it’s evolving, and whether there are other associated symptoms.

    The presence of systemic symptoms and signs

    Unlike herpes, a vaginal yeast infection doesn’t cause systemic symptoms and signs (overall weakness, flu-like signs, lymph node enlargement).

    The appearance of skin lesions

    • Herpes causes fluid-filled blisters or sores that are usually clustered on the same red base.
    • Vaginal yeast infection causes redness and swelling of the entire area.

    The presence of pain

    • Unlike herpes lesions (which are painful), a vaginal yeast infection is burning and itchy.

    The discharge

    • A vaginal yeast infection can have a thick, clumpy white discharge.
    • Herpes discharge is scanty and usually clear.
