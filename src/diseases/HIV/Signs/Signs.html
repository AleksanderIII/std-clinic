Early HIV signs and symptoms

Different terms, such as “recent,” “acute,” “primary,” and “early” are used interchangeably to describe the same thing: symptoms of HIV that appear within six months after acquiring it. The official scientific name for them is acute retroviral syndrome.


This section contains concise information on the most common early HIV symptoms and signs.


Let’s start!

Common HIV sign and symptoms

Acute HIV infection typically occurs within a few weeks after exposure to HIV-infected bodily fluids. This period associated with acute illness is called acute retroviral syndrome. This early HIV stage is very similar to flu or mononucleosis. With acute retroviral syndrome signs and symptoms usually very nonspecific and severity can range from mild to severe.


Here is a summary of the frequency of specific symptoms:


  • 75% of people get fever
  • 70% of people experience profound fatigue
  • 50% of people experience malaise and lethargy
  • 50% of people have generalized muscle ache
  • 50% of people present with rashes that can mimic drug allergies and other rashes
  • 50 % of people get sore throat without tonsillar white patches but with neck lymph node enlargement
  • 50% of people get headache
  • 30% to 40% of people get enlarged lymph nodes, primarily but not exclusively in the neck
  • 20% of people get diarrhea
  • 10% to 20% of people get oral sores, which is one of the most HIV-specific signs

Frequently Asked Questions

In this section, our expert doctor will answer real-life patient questions about chlamydia signs. Most questions are grouped to make it easier to cover more topics.

Dr. Fuzayloff has been a practicing physician at a busy STD center in Midtown Manhattan , New York City, for over two decades.

  • How common are early HIV symptoms

    Among people who have contracted HIV, 10% to 60% have no symptoms.

  • How soon should early HIV symptoms be expected?

    The average time from contracting HIV to having symptoms is two to four weeks.

  • What are the most common symptoms with early HIV infection?

    Fever, fatigue, and muscle aches are considered the most common symptoms of early HIV. Since most symptoms of early HIV are nonspecific (meaning other conditions can have the same symptoms), paying attention to the severity and duration of the symptoms should help differentiate HIV from other conditions.


    If you answer yes to any of these questions, the likelihood of acute retroviral HIV syndrome is higher:

    • Are your symptoms unusually severe?
    • Have they lasted for a long time?
    • Are they not responding to the sort of medication you usually take for these kinds of symptoms?

  • How high does body temperature goes with early HIV symptoms?

    The range is 100-104 F

  • What are the most specific signs and symptoms for early HIV?

    There is no symptom or sign that is specific for early HIV. Most early HIV symptoms and signs can occur with other very common conditions such as flu, mono, herpes, etc.


    A combination of these symptoms makes early HIV likely:

    • Nonspecific symptoms (fever, chills, fatigue, muscle aches, diarrhea, weight loss, etc.)
    • Prolonged duration and increased severity of nonspecific symptoms
    • Painful mouth, penis, and rectal sores

    The last two points are the most important to formulating an assumption of early HIV, with painful ulcers being the most suggestive.

  • What else can you tell me about the signs of symptoms of early HIV?

    Lymph nodes enlargement

    Lymph nodes usually become enlarged in the neck and armpits. Usually, they are not painful. They become enlarged during the second week of the illness and get better over time. There is a chance of their remaining slightly enlarged thereafter.

    Sore throat

    Sore throat is a very common symptom of early HIV infection. The throat can get red, but usually there are no white patches or significant tonsillar swelling.

    Rash

    Rash is common in early HIV, and it can cover the entire body, including the palms, soles, and scalp. The most commonly affected areas are the neck and chest. The rash is mildly itchy.


    The rash usually appears 48 hours after onset of the fever and self-resolves in a week’s time. It is usually well-defined red or pink spots.

    Painful sores

    Painful sores in the mouth, anus, or esophagus or on the penis are the most distinctive feature of HIV. Sores are usually shallow with well-defined borders and a white base.

    Diarrhea, nausea, and weight loss

    A lot of people with acute HIV infection get diarrhea, a suppressed appetite, and weight loss. The average weight loss is 6 kg or 13 lb

    Headaches

    It is common to have a headache with acute HIV—typically a frontal headache that worsens with eye movements.
